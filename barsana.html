Barsana

Shri Radha Rani Temple

The town holds cultural and religious significance as it is the birthplace and home of the Hindu goddess Radha, the chief consort of Krishna. Barsana is situated in the Braj region.Shri Radha Rani Mandir is the first temple of Shrimati Radha Rani `the godess of Love` on the top of Bhanugarh hills. Barsana is her birth place, people call her Ladliji and Shriji therefore also known as Shriji Temple or Laadli Sarkar Mahal. Holi and Radhashtami are the world wide famous festival of Ladli Lal Mandir.

Radha Kushal Temple

Shri Radha Kushal Bihari Temple in Barsana, the first thought which may cross your mind is: how similar it looks to those forts of Rajasthan! You would hardly be wrong in assuming so, for although the Hindu temple is located in Barsana, Uttar Pradesh, it was built by Maharaja Madho Singh Rao of Jaipur. Located on the Bramhachal Parvat in Barsana, its almond coloured facade rises gracefully amidst the thick vegetation of Gahvar Van (a forest where Radharani would relax along with her companions).

Kirti Temple

A temple named Kirti Mandir has been established by Jagadguru Shri Kripalu Ji Maharaj on a 12,000 square feet area in Barsana. This temple has been named after Shri Radharani's mother, Kirti Maiya.The temple dedicated to Divine mother of Shri Radharani, Kirti Mayia Mandir is unique in it's own way located at Barsana, the only temple on the world having the glimpses of Shri Radharani in the lap of Kirti Mother.

Rangeeli Mahal

Rangeeli Mahal, founded in 1996, is one of the main centers of Jagadguru Kripalu Parishat, is located at Shri Radharani's eternal abode, Barsana. In describing the glory of Barsana dham, Jagadguru Shri Kripalu Ji Maharaj had said that within this Barsana dham resides the divine Barsana dham.Rangeeli Mahal, there is a large satsang hall with depictions of Shri Radha Krishna's pastimes all around the hall. There are also beautifully landscaped gardens, waterfalls and other attractions. A temple named Kirti Mandir has been established by Jagadguru Shri Kripalu Ji Maharaj on a 12,000 square feet area in Barsana.
